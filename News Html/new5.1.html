Precure Miracle Leap Film Teases Revelations With Video on Opening Day

Film about mysterious time loop opens on Halloween after COVID-19 delays

The official YouTube channel for the Precure magical girl franchise posted a new special video for the Eiga Precure Miracle Leap: Minna to Fushigi na 1-nichi (Everyone and the Mysterious One Day) anime film on Saturday. The video includes spoilers for the film:


The film's official website also announced on Friday that the film's character designer and animation director Nishiki Itaoka drew 14 sketches that are on display in Tokyo's Shinjuku Wald 9, Yokohama Burg 13, and Chiba's T-Joy Soga theaters.

The sketches feature 13 Precure girls from the last three series as well as the movie characters Miraculun and Refrain, as well as signatures by their respective cast members. In addition, the staff is holding a Twitter campaign to give away the 14 sketches.

14 theaters in the T-Joy chain throughout Japan hosted screenings that started at midnight or later on opening day, which is Saturday or Halloween.


Due to the new coronavirus disease (COVID-19), few films have had midnight screenings for most of this year, although Demon Slayer – Kimetsu no Yaiba – The Movie: Mugen Train is among the films that began adding late-night screenings at midnight or later.

Most local governments in Japan have "juvenile protection ordinances" against minors being in theaters after 10:00 or 11:00 p.m., so T-Joy did not allow minors under the age of 18 to go to these midnight screenings.

In addition, the midnight screenings did not hand out the "Miraculun Light" sticks that filmgoers get in other screenings.
